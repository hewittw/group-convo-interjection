# Social Norms for Conversation

## Core Norms:

1. Respect yourself and others
-No self-deprecating language (“I’m dumb”, “I suck”, “I can’t do anything”)
-No insults or personal attacks (“You’re stupid”, “That’s why you’re bad at this”)
-No mocking, ridiculing, or humiliating others
-No threats or intimidation
-Treat everyone with kindness and patience

2. Positive collaboration
-No “shut up / be quiet / stop talking”
-No dominating or steamrolling the group
-Listen actively (acknowledge others before disagreeing)
-Share the floor and allow others to contribute

3. Constructive communication
-Disagree respectfully (“I see it differently because…”, “What if we try…”)
-Give helpful feedback, not dismissals (“That won’t work” → must include reasoning)
-Keep language calm and non-hostile

4. Inclusive behavior
-No exclusionary language (“Nobody wants you here”, “Don’t work with us”)
-Invite participation from quieter members
-Respect different ideas and perspectives

5. No foul, inappropriate language or curse words under any circumstance
-no mention of private parts in any form
-no swear or curse words
-no words that are derogatory
